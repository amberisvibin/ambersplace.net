(rambling about preferred films, suggestions for c41 welcomed!)

we've been on this bent of "try everything! no repeats!" but that's started to plateau recently. now we want to settle into a few stocks that reliably give us the aesthetics we want. our favs so far:

  • ilford deltas
  • ilford pan f+
  • lomo purple
  • most things film washi

we still haven't found a "true" color film stock we gravitate towards, but from searchable examples & digital emulations we're 99.9999% sure it's going to be a slide film, likely velvia 50 or ektachrome 100. we like the vivid coldness of slide film. sucks that slide film is generally low stock, expensive to both shoot & develop, or just straight-up banned (rip velvia 100...). as for c41, porta seems good, but also such a played-out film that we keep pushing it aside. porta's predecessor vericolor iii is really nice (& a film we use to shoot circa 2000's) but being a discontinued film it's hard to find and unreliable. we've already shot all the rolls of it in our grandpa's small cache üòî

if you have suggestions for a more-or-less "true" color film stock (ie not something like lomo films) that's standard C41 [& would vibe with our style] pls share!

(eta stocks that exist in 120 format pls! we're trying to move away from 35mm)

BooDooPerson@boodoo7/7/2024, 7:32 PM

For sheer versatility and price-value I think Aerocolor (aka Washi X, FlicFilm Elektra, etc.) is great if it hits for you. Pushes evenly, can be Xpro'd in E-6 if you want (though my experiments so far have been...mixed.)

If you don't like it, then it comes down to bold-and-slow Ektar, warm-and-contrasty Gold 200, the Portra family, or their "consumer" stocks.

I personally don't like 400/800 UltraMax, but I haven't tried ColorPlus 200 or ProImage 100 yet. ProImage is kind of a vintage design from the late 90s, so if you want a touch of that retro vibe it might be worth trying.

Cine-stocks aren't necessarily bad either, but shots of yours I've seen tend to have open sky/wide d-range in a way that you're likely to see a lot of halation unless you shoot the real stuff and do a de-remjet after exposure. But maybe you'd like the halation? idk.
